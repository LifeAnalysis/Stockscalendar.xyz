# Terminal Filter Consensus — Photon, BullX, GMGN, Axiom (2026)

The four dominant Solana memecoin terminals — Photon, BullX, GMGN, and Axiom — converge on a near-identical filter stack for prefiltering trending and new-pool feeds. Operators copy-paste between guides because deviation costs money fast, so the consensus carries weight as a revealed-preference benchmark.

## Mcap and Age

- Snipe-entry mcap floor: $9,000 — below this, liquidity and price-discovery noise turn sniping into coin-flip variance. DerivedFrom [[raw/photon-bullx-gmgn-filter-settings.md]].
- Snipe age window: under 30 minutes from launch. DerivedFrom [[raw/photon-bullx-gmgn-filter-settings.md]].
- "Super Degen" tier: 0–48 hours since launch. DerivedFrom [[raw/photon-bullx-gmgn-filter-settings.md]].
- "Degen" tier: 0–72 hours since launch. DerivedFrom [[raw/photon-bullx-gmgn-filter-settings.md]].

## Holder Distribution

- Top-10 cap: < 30 percent — tighter than Nansen's 40 percent because terminal users trade earlier in the lifecycle. DerivedFrom [[raw/photon-bullx-gmgn-filter-settings.md]]; Contradicts [[nansen-mcap-liquidity-thresholds.md]] 40 percent ceiling (stricter-wins applies in our pipeline).
- Bundle concentration cap: < 10 percent. DerivedFrom [[raw/photon-bullx-gmgn-filter-settings.md]].
- Airdrop allocation: < 10 percent preferred, 20 percent absolute max. DerivedFrom [[raw/photon-bullx-gmgn-filter-settings.md]].

## Behavior Signal

- Holder-dump alert: > 10 percent of airdrop supply leaving recipient wallets is a distribution event and triggers exit. DerivedFrom [[raw/photon-bullx-gmgn-filter-settings.md]]; Supports [[top-holder-dumping.md]].

## Why This Matters

- Supports [[strict-prefilter-gauntlet]] top-10 30 percent gate — terminal consensus is the precedent.
- Predates our v4.0 mcap floor: terminal $9k snipe floor sits an order of magnitude below our $100k early-conviction floor because the two systems target different lifecycle stages — terminals snipe pre-graduation, we enter post-graduation.
- DerivedFrom revealed-preference reasoning: when independent operators converge on identical numbers under competitive pressure, the numbers carry more weight than any single guide.
